# PAGE 1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## Governing thought (action headline)

**Pixxel should prioritize PMFBY crop-insurance analytics via a SaaS-led hybrid model to capture a $102M India TAM by FY2028, generating ~$12M in domestic ag revenue at 12% share.**

---

## Client situation (3 bullets)

- Pixxel has **$95M** institutional funding, **3 Firefly** hyperspectral satellites operational, and a **₹1,200+ crore / 12-satellite** national EO PPP with IN-SPACe (consortium: Dhruva Space, PierSight, SatSure).
- Aurora analytics platform is live; company is fragmented across agriculture, mining, oil & gas, maritime — **no dominant monetization strategy**.
- Window is narrowing: Planet Labs expanding, ISRO/Bhuvan provides free baseline EO, domestic startups scaling — Pixxel must transition from **hardware-centric startup → profitable data/analytics enterprise**.

---

## Problem (one sentence)

Pixxel possesses world-class hyperspectral infrastructure but lacks a concentrated, scalable commercial strategy to convert India's agriculture opportunity into sustainable FY2026–28 profitability.

---

## Hypothesis tested

> PMFBY satellite index underwriting is the faster volume path; enterprise precision-ag SaaS is the margin path — best delivered through a **SaaS-led hybrid** that bundles Aurora with standardized imagery.

**Result:** Hypothesis **confirmed** on market size + regulation; **qualified** on competition (SatSure channel strategy required).

---

## Three recommendations

| # | Recommendation | FY2028 target |
|---|----------------|---------------|
| **1. Market** | Size and pursue **$102M TAM** (base) for satellite-based precision ag analytics in India — PMFBY sub-segment **$72M (71%)**, enterprise **$30M (29%)** | 9.18M adopted hectares @ $11.10/ha |
| **2. Vertical** | **Primary: PMFBY / crop insurance index underwriting.** Expansion: enterprise precision ag (horticulture FPOs, sugar, export grapes) from FY2027 | 8 states PMFBY; 25 enterprise accounts |
| **3. Monetization** | **SaaS-led hybrid:** 55% Aurora SaaS / 35% DaaS-ARD / 10% services by FY2028 | Blended gross margin **~58%** |

---

## So-what (Page 2)

- Market is **large enough to anchor a national strategy** but requires **share capture** — full TAM ≠ Pixxel revenue.
- **71% of TAM is PMFBY/insurance** — volume game; enterprise is margin game.
- Hyperspectral premium applies to **<15% of hectares** (horticulture, high-value export); multispectral suffices for bulk index monitoring.

## Channel strategy — SatSure paradox

SatSure is both **consortium partner** and **PMFBY analytics incumbent** (44 districts, 8 states; YES-TECH validated).

**Resolution:** Pixxel supplies **hyperspectral data + ARD layer**; SatSure delivers **YES-TECH-compliant analytics** — formal revenue-sharing in consortium agreement. Pixxel avoids building duplicate CCE optimization; captures DaaS volume + SaaS upsell to insurers directly where SatSure is not prime contractor.

---

## So-what (Page 3)

- **Enter PMFBY first** for hectare volume and PPP alignment.
- **Do not ignore enterprise** — FY2027+ for hyperspectral premium and margin defense.
- **Win condition:** 2 state PMFBY contracts signed by end FY2026.

# PAGE 4 — SAAS-LED HYBRID MAXIMIZES FY2026–28 PROFITABILITY

## Governing thought

**A SaaS-led hybrid (55% Aurora / 35% DaaS / 10% services) maximizes blended margin (~58%) while meeting PPP volume commitments — pure DaaS commoditizes; pure SaaS misses PMFBY scale.**

---

## Exhibit 4A — Unit economics comparison

| Metric | DaaS (ARD bulk) | SaaS (Aurora) | **Hybrid FY2028** |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Gross margin | 45% | 70% | **~58%** blended |
| Typical deal size | $1.2M / state-season | $300K / enterprise | — |
| CAC | $80K (tender) | $45K (ABM) | — |
| LTV:CAC | 30:1 | 17:1 | — |
| Payback | 2.0 years | 1.1 years | — |
| Scalability | Very high (ha) | Moderate (accounts) | — |
| Concentration risk | High (top 3 = 60%) | Lower (20+ accounts) | Mitigated by mix |

### DaaS example (PMFBY state contract)
- 2M ha monitored × $0.60/ha effective bulk rate = **$1.2M revenue**
- 45% gross margin = **$540K gross profit**

### SaaS example (enterprise account)
- 8 Aurora Enterprise users + imagery + custom analytics = **$300K ARR**
- 70% gross margin = **$210K gross profit**
